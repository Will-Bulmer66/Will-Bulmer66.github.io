    Apply for a waste carrier permit or get an exemption

    Businesses that move waste themselves or arrange for someone else to move, buy or sell waste must have a permit or exemption.

    From:
    Environment Agency

    Applies to England

    Contents

    1. Types of waste carrier permit
    2. What you need when you apply for a permit
    3. Waste carrier exemptions
    4. What you need when you register an exemption
    5. Apply for a permit or register an exemption

    Your business may need a waste carrier permit if you do any of the following: 

    • transport waste 

    • buy, sell or collect waste 

    • give instructions to someone else to transport, buy, sell or collect waste 

    • import or export waste out of the UK

    If you only transport certain types of waste, you may need to register an exemption instead. 

    Types of waste carrier permit 

    There are 7 types of waste carrier permit. 

    Controller permit 

    This permit is for carriers who do any of the following: 

    • decide where and when waste is collected and transported 

    • make decisions and arrangements for someone else to transport waste 

    • buy or sell waste 

    You need a Controller import-export permit if you also import or export waste out of the UK. 

    Transporter permit 

    This permit is for carriers who only transport waste, taking instructions from another business. 

    You need a Transporter import-export permit if you also transport waste into or out of the UK.

    Controller-transporter permit 

    This is for carriers who do the activities of both the controller and the transporter permits. 

    You need a Controller-transporter import-export permit if you also import or export waste out of the UK.

    Controller-transporter trades permit 

    This is for businesses that move less than 750 tonnes of their own construction and demolition waste each year. 

    What you need when you apply for a permit 

    You’ll need the following information: 

    • the approximate amount of waste in tonnes that you expect to transport each year during the life of your 3-year permit

    • name and contact details of the business owner, directors or partners 

    • details of any environmental offences they have committed 

    • a way to pay (for example, debit or credit card) 

    • details of technical competence, if applicable to your permit

    ID checks will be carried out to verify the identity of the most senior person or people in the business.

    Waste carrier exemptions 

    Some businesses may not need a permit. 

    You may need to register a waste carrier exemption instead of a permit if you move: 

    • specific types of waste 

    • limited amounts of waste

    There are rules and conditions for each exemption.

    You can register any of the following exemptions. 

    M1: Moving waste that your business produces in the course of its work 

    This exemption allows businesses that are not waste operators to take away the waste they produce during their work. The waste must be taken to an authorised facility or collection point. 

    The exemption does not cover construction and demolition waste or waste contaminated by asbestos. For this, you would need a permit.

    M2: Moving waste free of charge if you’re a registered charity  

    This exemption allows registered charities to take their own or someone else’s waste to an authorised facility, if they do not charge for the service. The charity must not be a waste management business.  

    M3: Moving waste extracted by mining or quarrying  

    This exemption allows businesses to move extractive waste such as rock, soil, tailings (waste solids/slurries), drill cuttings, and processed water. It does not cover any other type of waste from mines and quarries. 

    M4: Moving animal by-products for use or disposal  

    This exemption allows you to move animal by-products to an authorised facility. 

    M5: Moving waste collected under a deposit return scheme (DRS) 

    Waste moved under this exemption must be collected from a DRS return point. Waste items must qualify under the DRS regulations.  

    M6: Moving fly-tipped waste from land that you own or rent 

    This exemption allows landowners and tenants to remove waste that has been illegally deposited on their land. You must report it to the Environment Agency then take it to a permitted site.

    The land must be private land that is not used for any waste-related business. You must remove the waste yourself or instruct a family member or employee to do it.

    The exemption does not cover asbestos or liquid waste. 

    M6: Moving sludge waste 

    This exemption allows businesses to transport sludge from sewage plants and septic tanks. 

    What you need when you register an exemption 

    You’ll need the following information:  

    • the type and amount of waste you transport each year 

    • name and contact details of the business owner, directors or partners 

    • details of any environmental offences they have committed 

    • a way to pay (for example, debit or credit card) 

    ID checks will be carried out to verify the identity of the most senior person or people in the business.
